Overview

Purpose

The purpose of the website is to share information about Pokémons. Many Pokémons were created based on animals and objects, but people do not always know about them. I want to share information about some Pokémons that were created based on fish and other sea animals. The site will share the information in a simple and funny way, to help people get connected.

Audience

The audience for this website is people who do not know about Pokémons and would like to know to interact with a group who likes it. The focus is on parents and older adults who do not grow up in contact with the games or animes about Pokémon and have children, teenagers, and young adults around them who play and have fun with Pokémon content. Knowing about Pokémon could help them to share time with their children/teenagers/young adults when they are playing or watching Pokémon and be able to talk about it.

Content

Home page

Catching attention - Yes, real life inspires games more than we can imagine! Connecting with the audience - This phrase is not a surprise to people who are familiar with games, but for those who are not, it could really be. Inserting the subject 1 - Maybe you want to know more about how real life inspires games and Pokémon could help you to understand it better, they are a great example of how nature animals inspire games. Inserting the subject 2 - Pokémon creators got inspiration from nature animals to create them. There is a huge quantity of Pokémon, and they could be classified by groups. The most interesting are the ones which were inspired by marine animals, so we will present them here! Inviting to read 1 - When we think of the “marine” word, we also tend to think of “fish”. There are so many sizes and names of Pokémon inspired by fishes, that could take a long time to know all of them. So, we will present some of them here! Inviting to read 2 - Being a small or big animal is not a limitation to inspire a Pokémon. There is so much more to explore about marine animals, that we can’t just talk about fishes. I invite you to discover other species too! Motivating to use the website - These two topics are amazing to start a conversation with people who already played Pokémon or who want to start understanding this huge Pokémon world, so don’t care about your age, the content could be great for you =) Focusing on learning and sharing, this will certainly be a great opportunity for you!

Fishes

Here we present three Pokémon that were inspired by fishes. Pokémon 1 - Stunfisk An electric and ground Pokémon type has as its origin the Stargazer, a fish that buries itself in the sea bottom and can make some electric discharges when disturbed. Pokémon 2 - Lanturn An electric and water Pokémon type. Its origin came from the Footballfish and the dolphins. The fish has a light ball up on its head and the dolphin is related to Apollo, the Greek sun god who has the dolphin as his signature animal. Pokémon 3 - Sharpedo It is based on shark and ocean sunfish. Both have the skin like sandpaper. Its body also remembers a torpedo, giving him a “license” to be a very fast Pokémon. Inviting to learn more - Now you know more about Pokémon related to fish and you can talk about it with someone! If you would like to know more about marine Pokémon, go to the page “Other Marine Animals”.

Images for the Page 2

Other Marine Animals

In addition to fish, there are many super interesting Pokémon that were inspired by marine animals. Here we present three of them. Pokémon 1 - Mantine A Pokémon based on manta ray. Their sizes vary from 5.5 meters to 7.5 meters, being very large. In the anime, there as scenes where the Remoraid, another Pokémon fish, swims attached to the Mantine undersides. Pokémon 2 - Lapras This Pokémon resembles the Plesiosauria, an extinct marine reptile. According to science, this animal lived on Earth, between the Triassic and Cretaceous periods. Pokémon 3 - Horsea Based on the seahorse, it also looks like a dragon. This similarity is derived from the Japanese legend which tells that when a seahorse lives for a century it becomes a dragon. Inviting to learn more - Now you know more about Pokémon related to marine animals and you can talk about it with someone! If you would like to know more about marine Pokémon, go to the page “Fishes”.
